Intellectual Property

Protect your ideas, innovations, and creative assets

Home / Practice Areas / Intellectual Property

When you create art, a product or an idea, you deserve the credit and the rights. I handle intellectual property cases involving patents, copyrights, trademarks and trade secrets. Your ideas matter. I help clients protect their creative assets—trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets—with proactive legal protection.

Intellectual property represents some of your most valuable business assets, yet it's often the most vulnerable to theft, misuse, or infringement. Whether you're an inventor, artist, entrepreneur, or established business, protecting your intellectual property is essential to maintaining your competitive advantage and maximizing the value of your innovations.

At Seattle Legal Advisor, I help clients identify, protect, and enforce their intellectual property rights across all major categories. From trademark registration and copyright protection to trade secret policies and licensing agreements, I provide comprehensive IP counsel that grows with your business.

Protect Your Creative Assets

Your intellectual property is often your most valuable asset. Don't let competitors steal your ideas or dilute your brand—protect what you've created with comprehensive IP strategies.

Trademark Protection

Trademarks protect your brand identity and prevent consumer confusion in the marketplace:

  • Trademark Search and Clearance: Comprehensive searches to avoid conflicts
  • Federal Trademark Registration: USPTO application and prosecution
  • State Trademark Registration: Washington state protection
  • Trademark Maintenance: Renewals and ongoing compliance
  • Trademark Enforcement: Opposition, cancellation, and infringement actions
  • Brand Protection Strategy: Domain names and international considerations
  • Licensing Agreements: Monetizing your trademark assets

What Can Be Trademarked

  • Business names and logos
  • Product and service names
  • Slogans and taglines
  • Colors and design elements
  • Sounds and jingles
  • Product packaging and trade dress

Copyright Protection

Copyrights protect original works of authorship and creative expression:

  • Copyright Registration: Federal registration with the U.S. Copyright Office
  • Work-for-Hire Agreements: Clarifying ownership of commissioned works
  • Copyright Assignment and Licensing: Transferring and monetizing rights
  • DMCA Compliance: Digital Millennium Copyright Act procedures
  • Fair Use Analysis: Understanding permitted uses
  • Copyright Infringement: Enforcement and defense strategies
  • International Copyright: Protection in foreign jurisdictions

Copyrightable Works

  • Written works (books, articles, website content)
  • Software code and applications
  • Music and sound recordings
  • Visual works (photography, artwork, graphics)
  • Videos and multimedia content
  • Architectural designs

Trade Secrets

Trade secrets protect confidential business information that provides competitive advantage:

  • Trade Secret Identification: Cataloging valuable confidential information
  • Protection Policies: Employee and contractor confidentiality agreements
  • Access Controls: Physical and digital security measures
  • Employee Training: Education about confidentiality obligations
  • Misappropriation Actions: Enforcement against theft or misuse
  • Exit Procedures: Protecting secrets when employees leave

Common Trade Secrets

  • Manufacturing processes and formulas
  • Customer lists and databases
  • Pricing strategies and market analyses
  • Research and development projects
  • Business plans and financial information
  • Software algorithms and source code

  • Prior Art Analysis: Assessing patent validity

IP Due Diligence

Comprehensive IP analysis for business transactions and investments:

  • Ownership verification and chain of title
  • Freedom to operate analysis
  • Infringement risk assessment
  • Licensing agreement review
  • IP portfolio optimization recommendations

Digital and Internet IP Issues

Modern IP protection requires understanding digital challenges:

  • Domain Name Disputes: UDRP proceedings and cybersquatting
  • Social Media Protection: Protecting brands on digital platforms
  • Online Infringement: E-commerce and marketplace enforcement
  • Website Terms of Use: Protecting digital content and data
  • App Store Issues: Mobile application IP protection
  • SEO and Trademark: Search engine optimization concerns

International IP Protection

Expanding globally requires international IP strategy:

  • Madrid Protocol trademark filings
  • Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) applications
  • Foreign copyright registration
  • International licensing and distribution
  • Anti-counterfeiting strategies
  • Import/export compliance

IP Enforcement and Litigation

When your IP rights are violated, swift action is essential:

  • Cease and Desist Letters: Cost-effective initial enforcement
  • Settlement Negotiations: Resolving disputes efficiently
  • Federal Court Litigation: Trademark and copyright enforcement
  • Opposition and Cancellation: USPTO proceedings
  • Customs Enforcement: Stopping counterfeit imports
  • Damages and Injunctions: Maximizing recovery
