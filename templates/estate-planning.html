Estate Planning

Protect your legacy and provide for your loved ones

Home / Practice Areas / Estate Planning

For individuals and families, I provide estate planning services including wills, trusts, and powers of attorney—so your future is protected. Estate planning isn't just about what happens after you're gone—it's about protecting your family, preserving your assets, and ensuring your wishes are respected throughout your lifetime and beyond.

At Seattle Legal Advisor, I help individuals and families create comprehensive estate plans that provide peace of mind and financial security. Whether you're just starting a family, building wealth, or planning retirement, proper estate planning protects your loved ones and maximizes the value of what you've worked so hard to build.

I work with clients to develop estate plans that reflect their unique circumstances, family dynamics, and financial goals—from basic wills and powers of attorney to sophisticated trust structures and business succession planning.

Secure Your Family's Future

Estate planning isn't just for the wealthy—it's for anyone who wants to protect their family, avoid probate complications, and ensure their assets go where they intend. Don't leave your family's future to chance.

Essential Estate Planning Documents

A comprehensive estate plan typically includes several key documents:

Wills

  • Last Will and Testament: Direct distribution of assets and appoint guardians for minor children
  • Pour-Over Wills: Work with trusts to catch assets not transferred during lifetime
  • Simple Wills: Basic asset distribution for straightforward estates
  • Complex Wills: Detailed provisions for blended families, business ownership, and charitable giving
  • Mutual Wills: Coordinated planning for married couples
  • Will Updates: Regular revisions to reflect life changes

Trusts

  • Revocable Living Trusts: Avoid probate and provide incapacity planning
  • Irrevocable Trusts: Tax benefits and asset protection strategies
  • Charitable Trusts: Support causes while reducing tax burden
  • Special Needs Trusts: Provide for disabled beneficiaries without affecting government benefits
  • Generation-Skipping Trusts: Transfer wealth to grandchildren tax-efficiently
  • Asset Protection Trusts: Shield assets from creditors and lawsuits

Powers of Attorney

  • Financial Power of Attorney: Authorize someone to handle financial decisions
  • Healthcare Power of Attorney: Appoint medical decision-makers
  • Limited Powers of Attorney: Grant specific, temporary authority
  • Durable Powers of Attorney: Remain effective during incapacity
  • Springing Powers of Attorney: Activate only upon specific events

Healthcare Directives

  • Living Wills: Document end-of-life care preferences
  • Healthcare Directives: Comprehensive medical care instructions
  • HIPAA Authorizations: Allow access to medical information
  • DNR Orders: Do not resuscitate directives
  • Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment (POLST): Detailed medical care instructions

Tax-Efficient Estate Planning

Proper planning can significantly reduce the tax burden on your estate and beneficiaries:

  • Federal Estate Tax Planning: Strategies for estates over $12+ million
  • Gift Tax Planning: Annual exclusion and lifetime exemption strategies
  • Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax: Planning for transfers to grandchildren
  • Income Tax Planning: Stepped-up basis and income tax minimization
  • State Tax Considerations: Washington state-specific planning opportunities
  • Charitable Deductions: Tax benefits of charitable giving

Business Owner Estate Planning

Business owners face unique estate planning challenges that require specialized attention:

  • Business Succession Planning: Transfer ownership to family or key employees
  • Buy-Sell Agreements: Valuation and transfer mechanisms
  • Business Valuation: Discounts and valuation strategies
  • Key Person Insurance: Protect business value and provide liquidity
  • Management Transition: Ensure business continuity
  • Tax-Deferred Exchanges: Like-kind exchanges and installment sales

Family-Specific Considerations

Every family has unique dynamics that affect estate planning:

Blended Families

  • Balancing interests of current spouse and children from previous marriages
  • Qualified Terminable Interest Property (QTIP) trusts
  • Lifetime gifts to ensure fair distribution
  • Clear communication about inheritance expectations

Minor Children

  • Guardian appointments and backup guardians
  • Trusts for minor beneficiaries
  • Education funding strategies
  • Age-appropriate distribution schedules

Special Needs Family Members

  • Special needs trusts to preserve government benefits
  • ABLE accounts for disabled beneficiaries
  • Long-term care planning
  • Coordination with public benefits

Charitable Giving

  • Charitable remainder trusts
  • Charitable lead trusts
  • Private foundations and donor-advised funds
  • Charitable gift annuities

Asset Protection Strategies

Protecting your wealth from potential creditors and lawsuits:

  • Domestic Asset Protection Trusts: Self-settled spendthrift trusts
  • Family Limited Partnerships: Valuation discounts and control retention
  • LLC Structures: Business entity protection strategies
  • Homestead Exemptions: Primary residence protection
  • Retirement Account Protection: ERISA and state law protections
  • Insurance Strategies: Liability coverage and umbrella policies

Probate Avoidance

Strategies to minimize or eliminate the probate process:

  • Revocable Living Trusts: Primary probate avoidance tool
  • Joint Ownership: Right of survivorship arrangements
  • Beneficiary Designations: Retirement accounts, insurance, and payable-on-death accounts
  • Transfer-on-Death Deeds: Real estate transfer without probate
  • Small Estate Procedures: Simplified probate for smaller estates

Retirement and Long-Term Care Planning

Planning for your later years and potential care needs:

  • Retirement Income Planning: Social Security optimization and withdrawal strategies
  • Long-Term Care Insurance: Protection against care costs
  • Medicaid Planning: Asset protection and eligibility strategies
  • Life Insurance: Wealth replacement and estate liquidity
  • Annuities: Income guarantees and tax deferral
  • Healthcare Savings Accounts: HSA and MSA strategies

Estate Plan Maintenance

Estate planning is an ongoing process that requires regular attention:

  • Regular Reviews: Annual or biennial plan assessments
  • Life Event Updates: Marriage, divorce, births, deaths
  • Tax Law Changes: Adapting to new legislation
  • Asset Changes: New acquisitions and dispositions
  • Family Changes: Relationship updates and new beneficiaries
  • Document Storage: Secure storage and access instructions

Common Estate Planning Mistakes

Avoid these frequent pitfalls that can derail your estate plan:

  • Failing to fund revocable living trusts
  • Outdated beneficiary designations
  • Inadequate liquidity for estate taxes and expenses
  • Poor communication with family members
  • Overlooking digital assets and online accounts
  • Failing to coordinate with business succession plans
  • Not updating documents after major life changes

Digital Estate Planning

Managing your digital assets and online presence:

  • Digital Asset Inventory: Cataloging online accounts and assets
  • Access Instructions: Passwords and security information
  • Social Media Accounts: Memorial settings and account closure
  • Cryptocurrency: Digital wallet access and transfer
  • Online Business Assets: Websites, domains, and digital intellectual property
  • Cloud Storage: Access to photos, documents, and files

Estate Administration Support

Assisting families through the administration process:

  • Probate Administration: Court filings and asset distribution
  • Trust Administration: Ongoing trust management and distributions
  • Estate Tax Returns: Federal and state tax filing
  • Asset Valuation: Professional appraisals and valuations
  • Creditor Claims: Reviewing and resolving estate debts
  • Beneficiary Communications: Regular updates and distributions
