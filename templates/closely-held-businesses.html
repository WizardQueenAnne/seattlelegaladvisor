Specialized Private Company Counsel

Closely held businesses need more than standard corporate advice—they need counsel that understands the unique dynamics of private ownership and close working relationships.

Understanding Closely Held Businesses

Closely held businesses are characterized by:

  • Small number of shareholders or owners
  • Limited or no public trading of shares
  • Active involvement of owners in management
  • Family or close personal relationships among owners
  • Shared control and decision-making
  • Limited exit opportunities for owners

Common Challenges for Closely Held Businesses

Private companies face unique legal and business challenges that require specialized attention:

Governance & Control Issues

  • Balancing majority and minority shareholder rights
  • Decision-making deadlocks and dispute resolution
  • Board composition and voting arrangements
  • Management succession and leadership transitions
  • Compensation and benefit disputes

Ownership Transfer Challenges

  • Valuation difficulties for illiquid shares
  • Limited market for ownership interests
  • Transfer restrictions and right of first refusal
  • Estate planning complications
  • Tax implications of ownership transfers
  • Generational wealth transfer planning

Family Business Dynamics

  • Mixing family and business relationships
  • Employment of family members
  • Fairness between active and inactive family owners
  • Next-generation preparation and involvement
  • Conflict resolution between family members

Legal Solutions for Closely Held Businesses

I help closely held businesses address their unique challenges through comprehensive legal planning and documentation:

Buy-Sell Agreements

Well-drafted buy-sell agreements are essential for closely held businesses:

  • Triggering events: death, disability, retirement, termination
  • Valuation methods and dispute resolution
  • Payment terms and financing arrangements
  • Transfer restrictions and approval processes
  • Tag-along and drag-along rights
  • Non-compete and confidentiality provisions

Shareholder Agreements

Comprehensive shareholder agreements protect all parties:

  • Voting agreements and control provisions
  • Board composition and representation rights
  • Information rights and inspection privileges
  • Dividend and distribution policies
  • Preemptive rights and anti-dilution protection
  • Deadlock resolution mechanisms

Employment and Compensation Issues

Managing owner-employees requires careful legal structuring:

  • Employment agreements for owner-managers
  • Compensation and benefit arrangements
  • Performance standards and accountability
  • Termination procedures and severance
  • Non-compete and non-solicitation agreements
  • Equity compensation and incentive plans

Minority Shareholder Protection

Minority shareholders in closely held businesses need special protection:

  • Oppression Claims: Protection against majority squeeze-out tactics
  • Information Rights: Access to financial and operational information
  • Tag-Along Rights: Ability to sell alongside majority shareholders
  • Appraisal Rights: Fair valuation in certain transactions
  • Liquidity Options: Mechanisms for exiting the investment
  • Board Representation: Voice in company governance

Business Valuation Issues

Valuing closely held businesses presents unique challenges:

  • Lack of market comparables and trading history
  • Discounts for lack of marketability and control
  • Key person dependency and business risk factors
  • Asset vs. income valuation approaches
  • Professional appraisal requirements
  • Dispute resolution for valuation disagreements

Tax Planning for Closely Held Businesses

Private companies have unique tax planning opportunities and challenges:

  • S Corporation Elections: Pass-through taxation benefits
  • Section 1202 Stock: Qualified small business stock exclusions
  • Estate and Gift Tax Planning: Valuation discounts and transfer strategies
  • Buy-Sell Agreement Tax Issues: Ensuring tax-efficient transfers
  • Compensation Planning: Optimizing salary vs. distribution mix
  • Succession Tax Planning: Minimizing transfer taxes

Dispute Prevention and Resolution

Preventing and resolving disputes is crucial for closely held businesses:

  • Clear Governance Documents: Detailed operating procedures
  • Communication Protocols: Regular meetings and reporting
  • Mediation and Arbitration: Alternative dispute resolution
  • Exit Mechanisms: Voluntary separation procedures
  • Professional Facilitation: Business coaching and family counseling
  • Legal Intervention: Court remedies when necessary

Growth and Investment Considerations

Closely held businesses planning for growth need specialized counsel:

  • Raising capital while maintaining control
  • Bringing in new investors or partners
  • Professional management recruitment
  • Board of advisors development
  • Exit strategy planning and preparation
  • Strategic alliance and joint venture structures

Family Business Succession

Transferring family businesses to the next generation requires careful planning:

  • Next-generation development and training
  • Fair treatment of all family members
  • Active vs. passive ownership structures
  • Employment policies for family members
  • Governance evolution as the business grows
  • Family constitution and mission development
