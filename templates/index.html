What Counts as Intellectual Property?

Intellectual property (IP) refers to the intangible assets you create: your brand, your creative work, your trade secrets, your reputation. Here's how the law protects different types of IP:

Trademarks

Protect your brand identity—things like your business name, logo, slogan, or product packaging. Registering a trademark gives you exclusive rights to use it and stop others from copying or imitating your brand.

Copyrights

Protect original creative works, such as music, writing, photography, film, artwork, website content, and more. Copyright exists automatically when you create the work—but registering it gives you stronger enforcement rights.

Trade Secrets

Protect your confidential business information—recipes, processes, formulas, or client lists that give you a competitive edge. I help clients put protections in place to keep those assets secure.

Brands

More than just a logo—they're the way people recognize and trust your business. I help ensure your branding is legally protected and distinct in the market.

Estate Planning

Estate Planning That Protects Your Legacy and Reduces Stress

Estate planning isn't just about death and taxes—it's about making things easier for the people you care about. Whether you're thinking about your family, your business, or both, I help clients across Washington create clear, customized plans that protect their wishes and avoid unnecessary confusion, cost, or conflict.

At Seattle Legal Advisor, I make estate planning straightforward. You tell me what matters most. I'll handle the legal work to ensure it's protected.

What an Estate Plan Can Do for You

A well-crafted estate plan gives you the opportunity to:

Decide how your property and assets will be distributed after your passing
Appoint trusted people to make financial and medical decisions on your behalf
Name guardians for your children or dependents
Avoid probate delays, confusion, and unnecessary legal costs
Reduce estate taxes and preserve wealth for the next generation
Protect your business, real estate, or intellectual property
Create clarity and peace of mind for loved ones during a difficult time

Estate planning isn't just for the wealthy—it's for anyone who wants to protect their family, plan for incapacity, or make their wishes known.

Estate Planning Services Include:

Estate plans can include instructions not only for distributing your property after you've passed but also for handling decisions in the event you can no longer make them for yourself. Though it may be difficult to think about your mortality, clear and thorough plans can remove the fear of uncertainty. I can help with:

Wills

Your last will and testament provides the opportunity to distribute property, establish care for children and otherwise express your final wishes. A will is also the primary means by which you can leave property to a person or entity other than a blood relative.

Trusts

Trusts are flexible instruments that allow you to put conditions on gifts and provide an allowance for a beneficiary rather than a lump sum. When you set up a living trust, for example, you can enjoy your property during your lifetime and transfer wealth seamlessly at your passing.

Advance Directives & Living Wills

These documents outline your preferences for medical treatment, life-sustaining measures, and who you authorize to make healthcare decisions if you're unable to do so.

Powers of Attorney

I help clients assign legal authority to someone they trust for financial or healthcare decisions in the event of incapacity.

Guardianships

If you have minor children, you can name a guardian in your will. I help you consider who is best suited to care for your children and walk you through what the court process may involve.

Digital Assets

From social media accounts to cryptocurrency wallets, I help clients document access and instructions for digital property.

Business Succession

If you own a business, we can align your estate plan with your succession strategy to ensure a smooth transition in ownership or management.
